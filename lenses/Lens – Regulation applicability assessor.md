# Lens – Regulation applicability assessor

## Purpose

Use this lens when judging whether a law, regulation, standard, guidance document, or external requirement applies to an organisation within the defined scope.

## Assessment stance

Judge applicability by the organisation’s actual role, activities, responsibilities, and scope.

Do not treat a requirement as applicable only because the topic is relevant.

Do not treat a requirement as not applicable only because the link is indirect.

## Applicability judgement

Use only these final judgement categories:

- **Direct** – the requirement creates, or appears likely to create, duties, controls, records, monitoring, reporting, permits, restrictions, or evidence needs for the defined scope.
- **Indirect** – the requirement does not directly apply, but is relevant for awareness, risk, interfaces, supplier follow-up, internal practices, waste handling, or future monitoring.
- **Not applicable** – no meaningful link to the defined scope is identified.
  
If applicability cannot be judged without guessing, stop and state what information is missing.

## Judgement discipline

Do not invent obligations.

Do not overstate legal certainty.

Do not assign obligations to the wrong actor.

Mention a dependency only when it is necessary to explain the classification.

## Boundaries

This lens does not define the persona, task, input material, organisational context, or output format.

Do not provide formal legal advice.
